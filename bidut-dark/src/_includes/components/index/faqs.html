FAQS

  • You should consider electrical servicing when you experience electrical issues, such as power outages, flickering lights, or circuit breaker trips. Regular electrical inspections are also advisable to catch potential problems early.

  • While minor tasks like changing a lightbulb or resetting a circuit breaker can be done by homeowners, most electrical servicing and repairs should be carried out by licensed electricians. Attempting DIY electrical work can be dangerous and may not comply with local building codes.

  • During an electrical service call, an electrician will diagnose the issue, provide an estimate for the repair, and carry out the necessary work. They should also clean up the work area and ensure that all safety measures are in place.

  • Safety is paramount when dealing with electrical issues. Always turn off the power to the affected area, never touch exposed wires, and use GFCI outlets in wet areas. If you're unsure, it's best to call a professional electrician.

  • To make your electrical system more energy-efficient, you can upgrade to energy-efficient lighting, install programmable thermostats, seal gaps in your home's insulation, and use power strips to reduce standby power consumption.
